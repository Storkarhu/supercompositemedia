About

Steph Maj Swanson is a Swedish-Canadian filmmaker, multimedia artist, and AI critic. Steph is best known for creating the viral AI phenomenon Loab, a work that sparked global conversations about AI ethics and creative expression.

As a filmmaker, Steph directed Suicide III, a satirical short employing deepfakes and synthetic imagery to critique AI hype culture. The film screened internationally at festivals including Uppsala Short Film Festival, The Museum of the Moving Image (New York), Videomedeja Festival (Serbia), and others. Steph also curated Algorithmic Grotesque: Unravelling A.I. at Uppsala Short Film Festival, showcasing filmmakers examining algorithmic society through AI.

As a speaker and researcher, Steph has presented at major conferences including Chaos Communication Congress (Hamburg), DEFCON (Las Vegas), and institutions such as Tekniska museet (Stockholm) and Museum of Contemporary Art (Belgrade). Steph's critical writing on AI appears in Vice, Critical AI (Duke University Press), and other publications.

Still & Moving Images

Steph's body of video and still image work was described by Galleries West as "the merging of repulsive with beautiful."

Recent work includes a series of cover artwork for STEMcell, an artist collective and record label featuring established electronic artists from labels including Houndstooth, Samurai Music, Monkeytown, and Aus. For the STEMcell releases, Steph developed a unique artistic process: "To create the artwork, an AI model was pummelled by analog waveforms from optical sound-on-film, causing sound information to heavily distort the fabric of the image."

Film Curator

2024 Uppsala Short Film Festival - Steph served as a film curator for the Uppsala Short Film Festival, one of Europe's leading short film events. Steph curated and hosted the special program Algorithmic Grotesque: Unravelling A.I., showcasing filmmakers who examine algorithmic society through the lens of artificial intelligence. The selection explored themes including distorted gender norms, surveillance, and eugenics, and featured works by Eryk Salvaggio, Marion Balac, Ada Ada Ada, Ines Sieulle, Conner O'Malley & Dan Streit, and Ryan Worsley & Negativland.
