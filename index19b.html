About

Based in Uppsala, Sweden

Steph Maj Swanson (artistic alias: Supercomposite) is a multimedia artist, writer, and filmmaker whose work explores the complex relationships between artificial intelligence, creativity, and digital culture. Based in Uppsala, Sweden, she creates boundary-pushing work that challenges conventional narratives around AI and technology.

Loab™ - Global Cultural Phenomenon

Steph Maj Swanson gained international recognition for her story about the AI-generated cryptid Loab, which sparked global conversations about AI ethics and creative expression. Featured across major media outlets including Rolling Stone, Der Spiegel, Artnet News, BBC Radio, ABC, Forbes, The Atlantic, and Smithsonian Magazine.

Loab sparked some lengthy ethical conversations around visual aesthetics, art and technology.
Smithsonian Magazine
The thread, with its genuinely interesting and unsettling elements combined with the mythos of an actual demon lurking within AI algorithms, is something that will probably have the movie rights auctioned off to Netflix by the end of the day.
Forbes

The Loab phenomenon was featured in "Negative Capabilities: Investigating Apophasis in AI Text-to-Image Models" published in the journal Religions (June 2023), demonstrating scholarly impact in theological and AI studies.

To learn more about Loab, visit loab.ai or read the original viral Twitter thread.

Digital Art & Collaborations

Recent work includes art for STEMcell, an artist collective and record label featuring established electronic artists from labels including Houndstooth, Samurai Music, Monkeytown, and Aus. Her artwork appears on two STEMcell releases: "STEMcell feat. Sceptrum" and "STEMcell feat. Propus."

For the Sceptrum release, Steph developed a unique artistic process: "To create the artwork, an AI model was pummelled by analog waveforms from optical sound-on-film, causing sound information to heavily distort the fabric of the image."

Her body of video and still image work was described by Galleries West as "the merging of repulsive with beautiful."

In October 2024, she curated and hosted the special program "Algorithmic Grotesque: Unravelling A.I." featuring filmmakers exploring algorithmic society through AI, covering surveillance, distorted gender norms, and eugenics.

Current Project

Recently received a grant from Sveriges Filmförbund (Swedish Film Association) to direct a short film about two people with an erotic fixation on the invisible algorithms inside cameras, exploring how computational photography shapes our self-perception.
