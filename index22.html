Visual Art

LOAB™

Steph Maj Swanson gained international recognition as creator of the Loab cryptid. Steph's story about the character sparked global conversations about AI ethics and creative expression. Featured across major media outlets including Rolling Stone, Der Spiegel, Artnet News, BBC Radio, ABC, Forbes, The Atlantic, and Smithsonian Magazine.

Loab sparked some lengthy ethical conversations around visual aesthetics, art and technology.

The thread, with its genuinely interesting and unsettling elements combined with the mythos of an actual demon lurking within AI algorithms, is something that will probably have the movie rights auctioned off to Netflix by the end of the day.

Loab might seem like standard creepypasta, but her origins are a whole lot more mysterious.
Rolling Stone

Still & Moving Images

Her body of video and still image work was described by Galleries West as "the merging of repulsive with beautiful."

Recent work includes a series of cover artwork for STEMcell, an artist collective and record label featuring established electronic artists from labels including Houndstooth, Samurai Music, Monkeytown, and Aus. For the STEMcell releases, Steph developed a unique artistic process: "To create the artwork, an AI model was pummelled by analog waveforms from optical sound-on-film, causing sound information to heavily distort the fabric of the image."

Film

Director & Writer

Suicide III - Original short film written and directed by Steph Maj Swanson for The Algorithmic Resistance Research Group (ARRG!) video art installation at DEFCON 31 Las Vegas (August 2023). The work employs deepfakes and synthetic imagery to construct a satirical counter-narrative to AI hype culture.

Film Curator

2024 Uppsala Short Film Festival — Steph served as a film curator and reviewer for the Uppsala Short Film Festival, one of Europe's leading short film events.

At the 2024 edition, Steph curated and hosted the special program Algorithmic Grotesque: Unravelling A.I., showcasing filmmakers who examine algorithmic society through the lens of artificial intelligence. The selection explored themes including distorted gender norms, surveillance, and eugenics, and featured works by Eryk Salvaggio, Marion Balac, Ada Ada Ada, Ines Sieulle, Conner O'Malley & Dan Streit, and Ryan Worsley & Negativland.
