SUPERCOMPOSITE MEDIA

Steph Maj Swanson f.k.a. Supercomposite is principal of Supercomposite Media, a multimedia practice based in Uppsala, Sweden, specializing in screenwriting, filmmaking, digital content creation, and critical AI discourse. Currently accepting new projects and creative collaborations globally.

Suicide III

Original short film written and directed by Steph Maj Swanson for ARRG!'s video art installation at DEF CON 31 Las Vegas (2023). The work employs deepfakes and synthetic imagery to construct a satirical counter-narrative to AI hype culture. The film has screened at The Museum of the Moving Image New York (Philip K Dick Film Festival, April 2024), Uppsala Short Film Festival (October 2024), Videomedeja festival Novi Sad Serbia (October 2024), and at 101 Dump Gallery in Almaty, Kazakhstan.

Available for screenwriting projects, film development, and creative collaborations.

Uppsala Short Film Festival

Steph Maj Swanson previously served as Film Programmer/Reviewer at Uppsala Short Film Festival, Sweden's main arena for short film and one of Europe's most important short film festivals. Founded in 1982, the festival screens more than 300 short films annually and is recognized by the Academy of Motion Picture Arts and Sciences, meaning winning films may qualify for Oscar nominations.

In October 2024, Steph curated and hosted the special program "Algorithmic Grotesque: Unravelling A.I." described as "part educational, part descent into madness" featuring filmmakers who "put a critical eye to our algorithmic society, using A.I. to explore topics such as distorted gender norms, surveillance, and eugenics." The program included works by Eryk Salvaggio, Marion Balac, Ada Ada Ada, Ines Sieulle, Conner O'Malley & Dan Streit, and Ryan Worsley & Negativland.

Her own film Suicide III was also selected for the festival's "Film from Uppland" program, showcasing regional filmmaking talent.

Digital Art & Music Collaborations

Steph creates digital artwork for electronic music releases, collaborating with experimental artists and labels. Recent work includes art for STEMcell, an artist collective and record label featuring established electronic artists from labels including Houndstooth, Samurai Music, Monkeytown, and Aus. Steph developed a unique artistic process: "an AI model was pummeled with images of optical sound-on-film waveforms—until the model could no longer produce images without traces of analog audio information."
