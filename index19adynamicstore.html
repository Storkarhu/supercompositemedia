Visual Art

Loab™

Steph Maj Swanson gained international recognition for her story about the AI-generated cryptid Loab, which sparked global conversations about AI ethics and creative expression. Featured across major media outlets including Rolling Stone, Der Spiegel, Artnet News, BBC Radio, ABC, Forbes, The Atlantic, and Smithsonian Magazine.

Loab sparked some lengthy ethical conversations around visual aesthetics, art and technology.

Still & Moving Images

Her body of video and still image work was described by Galleries West as "the merging of repulsive with beautiful."

Recent work includes art for STEMcell, an artist collective and record label featuring established electronic artists from labels including Houndstooth, Samurai Music, Monkeytown, and Aus. Her artwork appears on two STEMcell releases: "STEMcell feat. Sceptrum" and "STEMcell feat. Propus." For the Sceptrum release, Steph developed a unique artistic process: "To create the artwork, an AI model was pummelled by analog waveforms from optical sound-on-film, causing sound information to heavily distort the fabric of the image."

Director & Writer

Suicide III - Original short film written and directed by Steph Maj Swanson for The Algorithmic Resistance Research Group (ARRG!) video art installation at DEFCON 31 Las Vegas (August 2023). The work employs deepfakes and synthetic imagery to construct a satirical counter-narrative to AI hype culture.

Curator & Programmer

2024 Uppsala Short Film Festival — Steph served as a Film Programmer and Reviewer for the Uppsala Short Film Festival, one of Europe's leading short film events. Established in 1982 and recognized by the Academy of Motion Picture Arts and Sciences, the festival screens over 300 short films each year.

At the 2024 edition, Steph curated and hosted the special program Algorithmic Grotesque: Unravelling A.I., showcasing filmmakers who examine algorithmic society through the lens of artificial intelligence. The selection explored themes including distorted gender norms, surveillance, and eugenics, and featured works by Eryk Salvaggio, Marion Balac, Ada Ada Ada, Ines Sieulle, Conner O'Malley & Dan Streit, and Ryan Worsley & Negativland.
